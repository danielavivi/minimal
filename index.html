Summary for my website

My name is Daniela Villacreces Villacis, and I am an aspiring PhD candidate in Economics with a strong focus on macroeconomic stability, international trade, and development. My research has explored diverse topics, including the effects of public debt and investment on economic growth, the role of manufacturing exports to developed countries in fostering export sophistication, and the welfare implications of fiscal policy rules in commodity-dependent economies.

I graduated as the top student in my undergraduate program at King’s College London and I earned a complete scholarship to study an MSc in Economics for Development from the University of Oxford, where I ranked among the top 5 students in my cohort. My academic journey reflects a commitment to rigorous analysis and a deep curiosity about the economic mechanisms that shape global and local outcomes.

This website provides comprehensive information about my academic background, research experience, and PhD application materials. If you have any questions or would like to discuss my work, please don’t hesitate to reach out.
